I was honoured in 2019 to be able to give my first full conference talk at [CodeMobile](http://www.codemobile.co.uk). I was then lucky enough to be able to repeat that talk at [NSLondon](https://www.meetup.com/NSLondon/), [NSManchester](https://www.meetup.com/NSManchester/), and [SWMobile](https://www.meetup.com/swmobile/) meetups.

As an iOS developer I know accessibility is important for a huge range of people. But at times I think I can treat it like an afterthought. Accessibility Without the 'V' Word covers a skill I think we as software engineers would benefit from developing - empathy towards our users. Remembering that everyone who uses our software is different, and have different abilities, experiences, and requrements. VoiceOver is an essential tool for many of our customers, but its not the only thing we should consider with accessibility.

YouTube hosts the [SWMobile talk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nv6YKcFtzv8) and the [CodeMobile talk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=majvme8fMvE) and I thank both groups for recording and uploading them.

Each version of the talk was slightly different, but if you would like to follow along grab the [CodeMobile slides](/resources/Without-The-V-Word.key).
